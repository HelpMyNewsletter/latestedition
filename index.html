Part 1

Word-of-mouth will still be king in 2026, but you are doing it wrong

The stat that matters: 42% of successful creators cite direct recommendations as their #1 growth strategy. Not ads. Not SEO. Not LinkedIn threads. People telling other people.

But here is what most of you miss: people do not recommend bland content. They do not share "5 tips" newsletters that sound like every other newsletter in their industry. They share stuff that makes them think differently, laugh out loud, or get pissed off enough to argue about it.

The data does not lie

HubSpot data shows newsletters with hot takes and strong opinions drive the highest open rates, click rates, AND conversion rates. Not "balanced perspectives." Not "here are some thoughts." Actual opinions that make people pick a side.

What this means heading into 2026

Stop writing like you are afraid of offending someone. Your competitors are playing it safe and getting ignored. The newsletters growing 3x faster than generic industry roundups have personality and perspective.

Make your newsletter so distinctive that people WANT to share it. Not because you asked them to. Because they cannot help themselves. Start this in January and by March you will see the difference.

Part 2

Local will be the new global in 2026 (and you are chasing the wrong audience)

Everyone wants to build the next Morning Brew. Massive audience, global reach, millions of subscribers.

Wrong game. Local and hyper-niche newsletters are printing money right now while you are fighting for scraps in oversaturated markets.

Case Study

LA Raver newsletter: 16,000 subscribers. $100k revenue. ONE YEAR. How? They focused exclusively on SoCal entertainment instead of trying to be everything to everyone.

The 2026 play

Stop trying to be everything to everyone. Pick a specific geography, industry vertical, or interest area. Own that space completely.

Why it works: Lower competition. Higher engagement. Easier monetization. A sponsor targeting SoCal entertainment venues will pay MORE to reach 16k local subscribers than to reach 100k scattered globally.

If you are trying to serve "entrepreneurs" or "marketers" or "business owners" you are already losing to someone who serves "solo real estate agents in Phoenix under 35" or "fintech compliance officers at Series B startups."

Strategic partnerships (the growth hack nobody is talking about)

Partnership newsletters grew subscribers 2.5x faster than solo efforts in 2024. Yet most of you are still grinding alone wondering why growth is so hard.

How it works

Find 3-5 newsletters with similar but not identical audiences. Cross-recommend each other. Split the cost of a joint lead magnet or webinar. Share subscriber acquisition costs.
